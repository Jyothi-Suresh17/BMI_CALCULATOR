What is BMI?

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a numerical value of a person's weight in relation to their height. It is a widely used screening tool to categorize individuals into different weight status categories, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

Here's how the BMI categories are generally interpreted:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
  • Obese: BMI of 30 or greater
While BMI is a convenient and widely used measure, it does have limitations. It doesn't directly measure body fat percentage or distribution, and it may not be accurate for individuals with a high muscle mass. It's a general screening tool, and a healthcare professional may use additional assessments for a more comprehensive understanding of an individual's health.

Let's maintain a healthy Weight....

Maintaining a healthy weight is essential for overall well-being and can significantly impact your health in positive ways. Always remember that achieving and maintaining a healthy weight is a gradual process, and small, sustainable changes over time are more likely to lead to long-term success. It's important to focus on overall health rather than just the number on the scale, and to adopt habits that contribute to a healthy and balanced lifestyle. If you have specific health concerns or weight management needs, it's advisable to consult with a healthcare professional for personalized guidance.
